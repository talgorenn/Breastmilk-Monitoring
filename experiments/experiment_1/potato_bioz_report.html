01 — Background

Experiment Overview

Bioimpedance (BioZ) measures the electrical impedance of biological tissue by injecting a small AC current and measuring the resulting voltage. The complex impedance Z varies with frequency due to the capacitive behavior of cell membranes and the resistive properties of intra- and extracellular fluids.

This experiment compares two potato samples — one dry (control) and one soaked in saline solution — using the MAX30001G analog front-end. A manual frequency sweep across 6 points allows fitting of the Cole electrical equivalent model to reconstruct the characteristic impedance spectrum.

02 — Setup

Hardware Configuration

The MAX30001G EV Kit was configured in bipolar 2-electrode mode: both sense (BIP/BIN) and drive (DRVP/DRVN) electrodes share the same physical contacts via the DP_BP and DN_BN jumpers. ECG electrodes were inserted directly into the potato flesh for stable contact.

Note: Bipolar measurements include electrode contact impedance in the total reading. A tetrapolar (4-electrode) setup would isolate tissue impedance alone. Despite this, the relative difference between samples is still clearly measurable.

Key observation: At low frequencies (125–250 Hz) both samples are nearly identical — the saline has not changed the low-frequency membrane resistance. At high frequencies (2–4 kHz), the saline sample shows dramatically lower impedance due to increased ionic conductivity from Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions penetrating the tissue.
04 — Analysis

Cole Model Fit & Cole-Cole Plot

The Cole model Z(ω) = R∞ + (R₀ − R∞) / (1 + (jωτ)^α) was fitted to the magnitude data using nonlinear least-squares optimization. The fitted parameters were used to reconstruct the full impedance spectrum and Cole-Cole plot (Re[Z] vs −Im[Z]).

05 — Conclusions

Discussion & Takeaways

The experiment successfully demonstrated the capability of the MAX30001G EV Kit to detect ionic composition differences in biological tissue through bioimpedance spectroscopy.

Saline lowers high-frequency impedance significantly: At 4 kHz, saline tissue showed 64.9% lower impedance than dry tissue, confirming increased extracellular ionic conductivity.
Low-frequency behavior is dominated by membrane capacitance: Below 250 Hz, both samples are indistinguishable — cell membranes block ionic current regardless of extracellular salt content.
α = 1.00 for saline indicates Debye relaxation: A single dominant relaxation time constant, consistent with a uniform ionic medium. The dry potato (α = 0.94) shows distributed relaxation from heterogeneous tissue structure.
Limitation — magnitude-only fitting: The MAX30001G outputs |Z| only, not phase. Cole-Cole reconstruction is estimated from model fitting rather than direct measurement. A device with phase output (e.g. MAX30009) would give a more accurate result.
